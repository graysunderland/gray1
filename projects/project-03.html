A two-to-three sentence overview that captures the heart of the project — the company's stage, the specific problem they brought to me, and the change that came out of the work.

This is the paragraph that decides whether someone keeps scrolling. Make it concrete: numbers, real stakes, the actual mechanic that mattered.

01 — The Problem

The interface had outgrown the model it was built on.

Two to four sentences describing the situation as you found it. What was breaking? Who was complaining? What was the business losing? Be specific — vague problems lead to vague case studies.

Optional second paragraph for context — competitive pressure, technical debt, team dynamics, whatever made this hard in a way that's worth talking about.

  • A specific symptom — quantified if possible.
  • A second symptom that points to a deeper cause.
  • A constraint that shaped the solution space.
